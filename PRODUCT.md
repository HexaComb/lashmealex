# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two audiences share equal priority:

- **Working lash artists** buying professional supplies. They know curls, lengths, diameters, and adhesives. They need fast variant clarity, accurate stock signals, and confidence that trays match salon-grade standards.
- **Beauty enthusiasts and at-home lash fans** exploring the category. They need approachable copy, clear product differentiation, and guidance without feeling talked down to.

Both shop on phones and desktops, often between clients or during short breaks. Local customers in Fresno may be optimizing for same-day pickup rather than browsing.

## Product Purpose

Lashmealex is the online shop for a Fresno lash artist's curated catalog: lash variants, adhesives, aftercare, and beauty tools. The site must make discovery and purchase straightforward (shop, product detail, cart, checkout) while reinforcing that every SKU is salon-vetted. Success means completed pickups and repeat orders, with admin surfaces that keep catalog, carts, and fulfillment reliable without stealing focus from the customer experience.

## Brand Personality

**Warm · Artisan · Trusted**

The tone is elevated boutique, not clinical wholesale and not playful novelty. Copy sounds like a practitioner who stands behind the shelf: specific, honest, unhurried. Visual luxury (reference: high-end beauty retail) supports the feeling that quality is worth the price, without cold distance.

## Anti-references

- **Cutesy kawaii beauty**: glitter overload, bubble fonts, infantilizing pink, sticker-style UI, exclamation-heavy microcopy.
- **Generic mass-market Shopify**: interchangeable theme layouts, rounded card grids, gradient hero blobs, discount-banner noise.
- **AI-slop patterns**: decorative glassmorphism, gradient text, side-stripe callouts, hero-metric dashboards on the storefront.

## Design Principles

1. **Serve both experts and newcomers on the same shelf.** Specs and variant selectors stay precise; headlines and supporting copy stay human-readable. Never hide pro detail; never gate it behind jargon.
2. **Credibility over hype.** Let curation, Fresno roots, and artist-backed selection do the work. Show what's in the tray; don't oversell with empty superlatives.
3. **Luxury through restraint, not ornament.** Premium feel comes from typography, spacing, photography, and materials-like surfaces, not from extra chrome or novelty widgets.
4. **Workflow-first surfaces.** Shopping, cart, checkout, and admin paths optimize for task completion (find variant, confirm pickup, fulfill order). Marketing moments support the path; they don't block it.
5. **Local trust is a feature.** Same-day Fresno pickup and salon pickup context should stay visible where they reduce anxiety, especially near purchase decisions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

**Best effort for now:** fix obvious contrast, focus, and keyboard issues as surfaces are touched; prefer semantic HTML and visible focus states. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` when adding animation. Plan for a formal WCAG 2.1 AA pass before major launches or compliance requirements.
